INSIGHT-LV

Discover novel biomarkers for cardiovascular risk prediction through the multimodal evaluation of left ventricle (LV) function.

  • Discover

Clinical Motivation

Heart failure (HF) affects over 26 million people globally. This project focuses on two high-risk patient groups: those with hypertrophic cardiomyopathy (HCM) and those affected by COVID-19. The goal is to enhance disease management and provide novel tools for better risk stratification.

Methodology

The project is structured around interconnected work packages:

  • WP1: Extraction of ECG and MRI biomarkers.
  • WP2: Combination of the biomarkers in AI predictive/explainable model.
  • WP3: HCM physiological model adaptation and personalization.
  • WP4: Integration of the computational and ML models.

Project Impact

Advancing the field of cardiovascular research and clinical decision support.

Scientific

Development of novel stratification methods integrating real-world data with physiological modeling, ML, and advanced signal processing.

Societal

Enhance patient outcomes, reduce unnecessary treatments, and increase healthy life years.

Economic

Lower healthcare costs by reducing hospital readmissions and the need for invasive procedures.

People

Dr. Marion Taconné specialized in biomedical engineering during her PhD and postdoctoral research. She completed her PhD at LTSI-Inserm 1099 at Rennes University, where she developed and applied hybrid computational modeling and ML techniques to evaluate cardiac function in echocardiography. She is currently a postdoctoral researcher at the B3Lab of Politecnico di Milano, Italy.
